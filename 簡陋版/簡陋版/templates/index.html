Arctic rabbit

Arctic rabbits are also known as mountain rabbits or blue rabbits. It is a rabbit adapted to the Arctic and mountainous environment, the arctic rabbit is generally 55 to 71 cm long and weighs 4 to 5.5 kg The ears and hind limbs are relatively small, the body is fat, tailless, and has a fluffy fluff that reduces the loss of heat energy to adapt to the Arctic environment.

arctic fox

Also known as the blue fox and the arctic fox, the body length is 50 to 75 cm, smaller than the general fox, and the tail length is 25 to 30 cm. The white fox is found along the coast of the Arctic Ocean and in the tundra of some islands, and can live on ice sheets at minus 50 °C. Loved nesting in the hills.

polar bear

Also known as the white bear or ice bear, it is a large carnivorous mammal produced in the Arctic region, and the polar bear is the largest surviving bear. It is also the largest surviving carnivore on land.
